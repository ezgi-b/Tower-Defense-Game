Tower Defense

by: Ezgi Bayraktaroglu

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I enjoy tower defense games a lot, like The Creeps or Tower Madness as phone apps, so I knew that I would enjoy making it, and that I had knowledge on how those types of games usually worked.

2. What I Learned

From user testing, I learned that the instructions were not very clear, especially to a person who hadn't ever played a tower defense game before, and did not know the objective. They also said that the difficulty increases too fast. Also, one user wanted there to be a faster way to spawn a tower, rather than clicking on the screen and typing the name of the tower they wanted into the prompt that popped up. One person said there was a strange bug where "there's a pretty interesting bug where the speed of the game is unrelated to the tower shooting delays." I encountered this issue sometimes while testing the game, but I could never figure out why it was like that.

3. Architecture of My Game

The class TowerDefense has a main method to run the game, and the class GamePanel creates the Panel that the game is displayed on and controls how the game pages change. The ObjectManager class managed all the other objects that were created by separate classes, like the enemies, the towers, the treasure chest, the projectiles. This class checked collisions of these objects, added new instances of those classes, controlled the score and the amount of money you had, and did other stuff too. It had ArrayLists of all the different objects that were used in the gameplay.

4. Technical Implementation

For my game, there is a path that enemies follow, and they are trying to get to your treasure chest in order to ransack it. You start off with a certain amount of money, and you can buy towers with it. Towers shoot projectiles at the enemies and eventually kill them, stopping them from reaching the treasure chest. There are different types of towers. One of them simply damages the enemies. The other sets the enemies on fire, so that the damage the first type of tower does is doubled. One part of this that I am proud of is the fact that the projectiles are basically heat-seeking. As the enemy moves, they follow the quickest path available to them at the moment. At first, the projectiles simply shot off in 4 different directions - up, down, left, and right. Then, I made it so the projectiles would go diagonally at 45 degree angles or simply straight, until they reached the enemy. Then I decided to use slope, or y/x to make the projectiles better. The slope was the units in the y direction the enemy was away from the projectile divided by the x units of the same way. The then used loops and the pythagorean theorem to make sure the projectile would move the approximate same distance each time. The projectiles slightly change the direction they are moving many times. One thing that didn't work with this system was when the projectile was supposed to got vertically. It took a long time to figure out. A vertical line has no slope basically, since there is no change in x, and you can't divide by zero. So I made it so that if the x value was zero, that the projectile would simply go up (or down, depending on the situation).

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part about this game was testing it over and over to see what my changes had done, and to make sure the game wasn't insanely hard, or impossible to lose. Since there were different approaches you could take to the game, I had to test them all to make sure you couldn't keep playing for ever, and that you would lose at some point.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

If I were to continue working on this game, I would like to change the difficulty level so that you could play for longer. I would also add better instructions, more towers, and a way to save the high scores from multiple players.
